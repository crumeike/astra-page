Rapid, accurate building damage assessment after tornadoes is critical for emergency response and recovery, yet automated methods struggle with the visual complexity of tornado-induced wreckage, severe domain shift, and extreme class imbalance. ASTRA is a large-scale framework that evaluates 79 open-source models (67 CNNs and 12 Vision Transformers) across 38 hyperparameter variations in 2,420 controlled experiments on the expertly curated Quad-State Tornado Damage (QSTD) benchmark of 5,517 street-view images annotated under the IN-CORE engineering taxonomy. The study disentangles the roles of architecture, optimization, and pre-training source, and validates cross-event generalization on the held-out Tuscaloosa-Moore Tornado Damage (TMTD) dataset.
